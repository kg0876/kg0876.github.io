Chapter 02 · Project One

Digital Banking Platform Upgrade

Done
PRJ-2026-0147 · Northbridge Financial Group (fictional) · $1.26M · 12 months · OSFI B-13
Charter→MS Project schedule→D365 actuals→RAID & change control→EAC forecast→Power BI pack

Charter at a glance

ClientNorthbridge Financial Group — retail digital banking platform, 8 years old DriverNo real-time payments · 12-week release cycle · fails OSFI B-13 technology-risk expectations · under-35 attrition rising ObjectiveUpgrade to API-first architecture — real-time payments, release cycle 12 weeks → 2 weeks, B-13 evidence accepted Budget$1,260,000 (BAC) · cost centre CC-4410 · 5.4% contingency My roleProject Control Officer — schedule, cost, RAID, reporting

The toolchain — where each system sits

MS ProjectWBS, dependencies, 6 milestones, baseline — the plan Dynamics 365Vendors, POs, invoices — the AP transaction export is where actuals come from ExcelRAID · change control · actions · decisions · the EAC cost model Power BIThe interactive steering pack

The story (STAR)

Situation$1.26M platform upgrade, month 8 of 12. YTD spend was dead on plan — 0.1% variance. It would have been easy to report Green.
My roleBaselined the schedule in MS Project, pulled actuals monthly from D365, ran RAID and change control, and built the forecast at completion — Actual + Committed + Estimate to Complete.
HurdlesThe AP export hid four defects: a duplicate invoice, an invoice with no PO reference (policy breach), $28K of unposted invoices understating the month, and a USD invoice needing conversion. And Contractor Labour was running 22% over plan.
ResultAdding $214,600 of committed POs, the forecast landed $31,461 OVER budget — not Green at all. Reported AMBER, took a contingency drawdown to the sponsor (CR-003), and renegotiated the contractor rate card. Reporting Green on YTD alone would have been true, and misleading.

Chapter 04 · Project Three

D365 Finance Implementation

In Development
PRJ-03 · IT / ERP · Halton Technology Services (fictional) · $850K · 6 months · full SDLC
Charter→Design sign-off→Build / migration→Test / UAT→Deploy / cutover→Hypercare

Charter at a glance

ClientHalton Technology Services — ~400-person IT firm Problem12-day month-end close, email invoice approvals with no audit trail, no project-level reporting. ObjectiveImplement D365 Finance as the single system for GL, AP & vendors — close cut 12 → 5 days, approval trail on every invoice. Budget$850K · 6 months · partner-delivered My roleClient-side PCO across the full SDLC

SDLC — what a PCO does in each phase

DesignTrack design-document sign-offs (unsigned design = the #1 cause of later disputes). BuildOwn the 3 data-load reconciliations — vendor master, open AP, GL opening balances. Test / UATMaintain the test & defect tracker; chase business sign-offs before go/no-go. DeployOwn the cutover runbook — minute-by-minute plan, owners, rollback triggers. HypercareRun the post-go-live issue log; track severity-1 defects to zero.

The story (STAR)

SituationA ~400-person firm on aging finance software: 12-day close, no invoice audit trail. Fix: implement D365 Finance — board-announced go-live.
My roleClient-side PCO across the full SDLC: design sign-offs, 3 data-load reconciliations, UAT tracker, cutover runbook, hypercare — plus the D365 flows UAT validates, executed hands-on.
HurdlesVendor master ~18% duplicates (incl. same-vendor-different-ID); GL opening balances didn't balance (suspense line — blocked go-live); key AP clerk resigned mid-UAT; partner wanted to compress UAT — pushed back via CR with defect-rate evidence.
ResultScenario: go-live +2 weeks (sponsor-approved), zero sev-1 in hypercare, close cut 12 → 5 days. Hands-on D365 evidence in progress.
